Acronyms, Use of Periods
Periods are used in acronyms (i.e., “N.A.F.T.A.” — not “NAFTA” or “Nafta”). So:
N.A.F.T.A.
Not: NAFTA or Nafta
The reason for this is signal to the reader that what they are reading is, in fact, an acronym, as opposed to a word in all capital letters or a proper noun (though, many things represented by acronyms are, to be sure, also proper nouns). No additional period is added if the acronym is the last item in a sentence. As far as we are aware, the New York Times is one of the only major newspapers that also uses periods in acronyms.
