Sanctions Timeline

Introduction

This page documents the Western response to Russia’s invasion of Ukraine. The focus is exclusively on primary source material. In compiling these resources, care was taken to ensure each resource was archived. This has been accomplished via the following means:

  1. [O] Linking to the original document;
  2. [IA] The Internet Archive’s Wayback Machine;
  3. [AT] Archive.Today Webpage Capture (a.k.a. archive.ph, previously archive.is);
  4. [PP] Printing the sources to PDF documents and locally saving these printed PDFs;
  5. [HR] Recreating the content in our own barebones HTML files (which we refer to as “HTML Reprints”);
  6. [LP] Saving local copies of documents originally appearing as PDF documents (and also archiving these original PDF documents on the Internet Archive’s Wayback Machine); and
  7. Identifying places where the resources are posted in multiple places (e.g., an Executive Order posted on the White House’s website and subsequently published in the Federal Register), reprinted, or hosted parallelly, and archiving these resources in the same manner.

Monday, February 21, 2022

Russian President Vladimir Putin appears on Russian state television to deliver a speech in which he recognizes the “independence and sovereignty” of the Donetsk People’s Republic and the Lugansk People’s Republic, both of which are located in the Donbas region of southeastern Ukraine. See Address by the President of the Russian Federation (Feb. 21, 2022) [O/IA/AT/PP/HR/LP]

United States President Joseph R. Biden, Jr., signs Executive Order 14065, titled “Blocking Property of Certain Persons and Prohibiting Certain Transactions With Respect to Continued Russian Efforts To Undermine the Sovereignty and Territorial Integrity of Ukraine.” 87 Fed. Reg. 10293 (Feb. 23, 2022).

Wednesday, February 23, 2022

The European Union extends sanctions to cover all 351 members of the Russian State Duma (Legislature) who voted on February 15, 2022, to recognize the independence of the self-proclaimed Donetsk and Luhansk “republics.” Economic restrictions were imposed on the “republics,” to wit: a ban on imports, restrictions on trade and investments in certain economic sectors, a ban on supplying tourism services, and an export ban on certain goods and technologies. The E.U. also sanctioned 27 "high profile individuals and entities." The E.U. also froze the assets of the sanctioned individuals and entities and also banned them from travelling to or through the E.U.

  1. Council of the European Union Press Release 151/22, EU Adopts Package of Sanctions in Response to Russian Recognition of the Non-Government Controlled Areas of the Donetsk and Luhansk Oblasts of Ukraine and Sending of Troops Into the Region (Feb. 23, 2022) [HTML: O/IA/AT/PP/HR/LP] [PDF: O/IA/AT/PP/HR/LP].

Wednesday, March 2, 2022

The United States Federal Aviation Administration issued Special Security Instructions (SSI) prohibiting within the territorial airpsace of the United States all air operations with any connection to Russia or Russian nationals. The immediate effect of the SSI was the grounding and prohibition of all commercial Russian airlines and the personal jets of Russian oligarchs from the skies of the United States.
